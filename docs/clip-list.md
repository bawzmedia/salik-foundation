## Clip 1: The Norsemen
**Frames:** 0001–0120
**Era:** Scandinavia, 10th Century
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
A Viking longship cutting through a misty fjord at dawn. Cold northern light, breath visible in the air. Norse warriors stand on the deck or a rocky shore. Camera slowly pushes in or drifts laterally. Among the warriors — one holds an object (a ring, a coin, a piece of cloth) inscribed with Arabic Kufic script. The tone is discovery, mystery.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Slow lateral drift or gentle push-in toward the longship
- **Lighting:** Cold pre-dawn, pale blue-grey sky, low sun barely cresting mountains
- **Color Palette:** Steel grey, ice blue, dark weathered wood, cold dawn light
- **Key Elements:** Longship with square sail, fjord with steep mountain walls, fog/mist on water, Norse warriors in furs/chainmail, Arabic-inscribed artifact visible
- **Atmosphere:** Haunting, unexpected — Islam in the last place you'd expect
- **Historical Reference:** Viking silver coins with "Allah" inscriptions found in Sweden, Ahmad ibn Fadlan's account of meeting the Rus Vikings (921 CE), Arabic Kufic ring found in Birka grave

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
Ice and water begin dissolving — particles of frost transform into golden sand grains. The cold blue palette warms to gold/amber.

## Clip 2: The Empire of Gold
**Frames:** 0121–0240
**Era:** West Africa, 13th–14th Century
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
The grandeur of Timbuktu and Mansa Musa's legendary caravan. A vast procession crossing the Sahara — hundreds of people, camels laden with gold, the scale is staggering. Cut or morph to the Djinguereber Mosque and the Sankore University library filled with scholars and manuscripts.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Sweeping aerial/wide establishing shot of the caravan, then slow push into the architecture of Timbuktu
- **Lighting:** Golden hour, blazing Saharan sun, warm dramatic shadows
- **Color Palette:** Rich gold, deep earth brown, indigo fabric, sunset amber, terracotta
- **Key Elements:** Massive caravan with hundreds of camels, gold bars/dust visible, Mansa Musa in royal garb, Djinguereber Mosque's distinctive mud-brick architecture, scholars with manuscripts
- **Atmosphere:** Awe, power, opulence — a civilization most have never heard of
- **Historical Reference:** Mansa Musa's 1324 hajj with 60,000 men, 12,000 slaves each carrying 4 pounds of gold, crashed Mediterranean gold markets. Timbuktu had 25,000 students. Columbus's son documented finding Mandinka spear tips in the Americas.

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
Gold dust and sand particles swirl upward, catching light, then reform as ocean spray — waves forming across the frame, transitioning to the blue-green of the South China Sea.

## Clip 3: The Silk Road & the Middle Kingdom
**Frames:** 0241–0360
**Era:** China, 7th–14th Century
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
Zheng He's massive treasure fleet — 300 ships stretching to the horizon, dwarfing anything Europe had at the time. The lead ship is enormous (400+ feet). Chinese Muslim sailors on deck. Transition to the ancient Xi'an Mosque blending Chinese and Islamic architecture, or Silk Road traders moving through mountain passes.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Wide establishing shot of the fleet from above, slowly descending toward the flagship, or lateral tracking alongside the ships
- **Lighting:** Morning light on open ocean, or misty mountain passes with dappled light
- **Color Palette:** Jade green, imperial red, gold, ink black, silk white, ocean blue
- **Key Elements:** Massive wooden treasure ships with red sails, Chinese Muslim admiral in robes, the Xi'an Mosque's blend of pagoda and minaret, Silk Road caravans through mountain terrain
- **Atmosphere:** Scale, sophistication — shattering the myth that Islam was confined to the Middle East
- **Historical Reference:** Zheng He commanded 300 ships and 28,000 men, exploring SE Asia, India, Arabia, and East Africa decades before European "exploration." Huaisheng Mosque in Guangzhou built 627 CE. The Hui people are one of China's largest Muslim communities.

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
Ship sails billow and fragment — the fabric texture morphs into the intricate geometric patterns of Moorish tilework, the blue-green of ocean becoming the blue of Islamic tiles.

## Clip 4: The Moors
**Frames:** 0361–0480
**Era:** Al-Andalus & Europe, 8th–15th Century
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
The breathtaking interior of the Great Mosque of Cordoba — endless red and white horseshoe arches receding into perspective. Moorish scholars in the libraries of Toledo translating and preserving knowledge. The Alhambra palace courtyards at golden hour, water reflecting intricate carved walls.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Slow dolly through the forest of arches in Cordoba, or slow reveal of the Alhambra's Court of the Lions
- **Lighting:** Warm Mediterranean light filtering through arches, golden hour on marble and water
- **Color Palette:** Terracotta red, deep blue tile (zellige), white marble, olive green, gold filigree
- **Key Elements:** Cordoba's iconic red/white double arches, scholars with astrolabes and manuscripts, Alhambra's intricate muqarnas (honeycomb vaulting), flowing water in geometric channels, European students learning from Moorish teachers
- **Atmosphere:** Intellectual beauty, sophistication — the foundations of the Renaissance came from Muslims
- **Historical Reference:** 800 years of Muslim rule in Iberia, Cordoba had street lighting and 70 libraries when London was a muddy village. "Algebra," "algorithm," "chemistry," "zenith" all from Arabic.

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
Geometric tile patterns fracture and reform — angular shapes soften into organic forms, becoming tropical foliage and warm wooden textures of Southeast Asian architecture.

## Clip 5: The Island Archipelago
**Frames:** 0481–0600
**Era:** Southeast Asia, 13th–16th Century
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
Muslim traders arriving at a bustling port city — wooden trading ships docked in a tropical harbor. The Kampung Kling Mosque or similar traditional wooden mosque rising among lush greenery. Communities gathering for prayer at sunset over the Indian Ocean. Rice paddies in the background. The feel is organic, peaceful, human.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Slow pan across a harbor scene, or gentle push toward a mosque at sunset with silhouetted figures
- **Lighting:** Tropical golden sunset, warm humidity haze, rich saturated natural colors
- **Color Palette:** Lush green (rice paddies, jungle), ocean turquoise, warm teak wood, sunset gold/orange
- **Key Elements:** Wooden trading vessels (dhows/junks), tropical port with palm trees, traditional wooden mosque with multi-tiered roof, diverse traders (Arab, Indian, Chinese, Malay), community prayer scene
- **Atmosphere:** Peace, organic growth — the most powerful dawah is how you live
- **Historical Reference:** Islam spread across SE Asia through trade, not conquest. Muslim merchants married locally and demonstrated Islam through character. Indonesia — 230M Muslims — was never conquered by a Muslim army.

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
The ocean horizon line holds steady, but the warm golden sky desaturates — colors shift to sepia tones, the tropical scene fading into a more muted, historical quality, as if the image is aging before your eyes into an old photograph.

## Clip 6: The New World
**Frames:** 0601–0720
**Era:** Americas & the Modern Ummah, 15th Century–Today
**Duration:** 5 seconds at 24fps

### Scene Description
A montage transition — Malian sailors on Atlantic waters (wooden boats on open ocean), then African Muslims in chains yet praying (silhouetted figures in prayer position), then early mosques in North America, then rapidly morphing into modern diverse Muslim communities: a hijabi woman at a laptop, children in a classroom, a family breaking fast, a diverse congregation in prayer. The visual language shifts from AI art to photographic realism.

### Visual Direction
- **Camera Movement:** Cross-dissolve montage, each scene holding for ~1.5 seconds, gradually accelerating
- **Lighting:** Shifts from historical warm/sepia to modern natural light
- **Color Palette:** Sepia tones dissolving into vibrant modern color — the world literally coming alive with color
- **Key Elements:** West African boat on open ocean, silhouetted prayer in adversity, first American mosques, modern Muslim diversity (multiple ethnicities, professions, settings), city skylines with minarets
- **Atmosphere:** Resilience, arrival — the message cannot be stopped, it finds every people
- **Historical Reference:** Ferdinand Columbus documented Mandinka trade goods in the Americas. Bilali Muhammad and Omar ibn Said preserved faith and Arabic literacy while enslaved. Islam is now the fastest-growing religion worldwide.

### Transition (last 10-15 frames)
Montage accelerates through modern scenes — faces, cities, mosques — then sudden stillness. The image brightens, washing out to near-white, creating a blank canvas for Era 7.

## Technical Requirements

| Spec | Desktop | Mobile |
|------|---------|--------|
| Resolution | 1920×1080 | 1080×1920 |
| Format | WebP | WebP |
| Quality | 80-85% | 75-80% |
| Target file size | ~30-50KB/frame | ~25-40KB/frame |
| Total frames | 840 | 840 |
| Total est. size | ~25-40MB | ~20-30MB |

### Compression Notes
- WebP with lossy compression
- Slight grain/film texture is fine — hides compression artifacts
- Avoid sharp text in frames (text is handled by code overlay)
- Ensure smooth motion — avoid jumps between frames
- First frame of each era should be a strong "hero" composition

### Batch Delivery
Deliver in 7 batches (one per era) for progressive integration:
1. `frame-0001.webp` through `frame-0120.webp`
2. `frame-0121.webp` through `frame-0240.webp`
3. etc.

## AI Generation Notes

For AI video/image generation (Runway, Kling, Sora, Midjourney, etc.):

- Generate as 5-second video clips at 24fps, then extract frames
- Use consistent aspect ratios (16:9 for desktop, 9:16 for mobile)
- Mobile frames can be crops/reframes of desktop — same scene, tighter composition
- Transitions between eras can be generated as separate short clips and appended
- Maintain cinematic film grain across all eras for visual cohesion
- No text, logos, or UI elements in the generated frames — all handled by code
