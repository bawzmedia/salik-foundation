## ERA 1: THE NORSEMEN — Dawah Through Trade
**Time Period:** Scandinavia, 10th Century
**Color Palette:** Steel grey, ice blue, dark weathered wood, cold dawn light
**Frames:** 0001–0360
**Dawah Thread:** Muslim traders on the Volga route shared the message with Norse merchants. The Qur'an's words literally traveled north — inscribed on silver, carried by believers who traded goods and shared their faith.

### Scene 1A — The Volga Trade Route
**Frames:** 0001–0120

A Muslim trader from the Abbasid Caliphate sits with Norse merchants at a river camp along the Volga. Between them are trade goods — silver, furs, amber. The Muslim trader has a Qur'an or scroll of Arabic text open beside him. The Norsemen lean in, curious. Firelight illuminates the Arabic script. The scene captures the moment of first encounter — the message being shared through trust built in trade.

- **Camera:** Slow push-in toward the group, settling on the open Qur'anic text
- **Lighting:** Warm firelight against cold northern dusk, faces lit by flames
- **Key Elements:** Muslim trader with Arabic text/Qur'an, Norse merchants listening, trade goods between them, firelight on calligraphy, river camp setting

### Scene 1B — The Inscription
**Frames:** 0121–0240

Close-up of Norse hands holding a silver ring. Camera slowly rotates to reveal Arabic Kufic script — "Allah" or "Bismillah" — inscribed on the band. The warrior turns it in the light, his expression one of reverence, not confusion. He has understood something. Behind him, the fjord and longships are softly out of focus. This is the evidence — the Qur'an's words reached even here.

- **Camera:** Macro close-up, slow rotation of the ring revealing the inscription
- **Lighting:** Cold sidelight catching the engraved Arabic script, making it glow
- **Key Elements:** Arabic Kufic inscription on silver, Norse warrior's hands, reverence in expression, fjord background soft

### Scene 1C — Ice to Sand Transition
**Frames:** 0241–0360

The Arabic letters on the silver begin to glow with warm light. The glow spreads — the cold northern scene dissolves into particles of light. Ice becomes golden sand. The calligraphy persists through the transition — the same Arabic script that was on Norse silver now appears written in desert sand. The message is the constant; only the land changes.

- **Camera:** Macro to wide pull, following the calligraphic thread
- **Lighting:** Cold blue transforming to warm gold, the Arabic text glowing throughout
- **Key Elements:** Arabic calligraphy as visual bridge, ice to sand transformation, the message persisting across landscapes

## ERA 2: THE EMPIRE OF GOLD — Dawah Through Scholarship
**Time Period:** West Africa, 13th–14th Century
**Color Palette:** Rich gold, deep earth brown, indigo fabric, sunset amber, terracotta
**Frames:** 0361–0720
**Dawah Thread:** Mansa Musa didn't just carry gold — he carried the Qur'an. He built mosques, funded Islamic universities, and made Timbuktu the intellectual capital of the world. The Qur'an was copied by hand thousands of times in these libraries.

### Scene 2A — Mansa Musa's Hajj Caravan
**Frames:** 0361–0480

The great caravan crossing the Sahara — but the focus is not on the gold. It's on Mansa Musa himself, riding at the head, carrying a Qur'an. Behind him, scholars ride alongside warriors. They are on hajj — this journey is an act of worship. Mosque silhouettes appear on the horizon of every city he approaches. He built mosques everywhere he stopped.

- **Camera:** Sweeping wide shot, then slow push toward Mansa Musa with Qur'an in hand
- **Lighting:** Blazing golden hour, dramatic shadows, dust catching sunlight
- **Key Elements:** Mansa Musa holding Qur'an, scholars in caravan, mosque silhouettes on horizon, hajj journey, gold as secondary element to the faith

### Scene 2B — The Qur'an Scribes of Timbuktu
**Frames:** 0481–0600

Inside the Sankore University. Scribes sit cross-legged, meticulously copying the Qur'an by hand. The camera moves across rows of beautiful Arabic calligraphy being written in gold and colored inks. Stacks of completed Qur'ans line the walls — thousands of copies. A teacher recites while students follow in their manuscripts. This was one of the greatest centers of Qur'anic learning in history.

- **Camera:** Slow tracking shot across the scribes' hands, then pulling back to reveal the vast room
- **Lighting:** Warm interior light, golden shafts through windows illuminating the calligraphy
- **Key Elements:** Hands writing Qur'anic text, gold ink calligraphy, stacks of Qur'ans, teacher reciting, students following along, the sheer volume of manuscripts

### Scene 2C — Gold Dust to Ocean
**Frames:** 0601–0720

Pages of Qur'anic text flutter in wind — the manuscripts being carried outward from Timbuktu. The pages dissolve into golden particles that swirl and spread across the frame, carried further and further. The gold particles descend toward dark water — becoming reflections on an ocean surface. The message is leaving Africa, crossing the sea.

- **Camera:** Following the pages as they scatter, then wide as particles meet water
- **Lighting:** Gold fading to deep oceanic blue, script still faintly visible in the water reflections
- **Key Elements:** Qur'anic pages scattering, calligraphy dissolving into golden particles, particles settling on ocean water

## ERA 3: THE SILK ROAD & THE MIDDLE KINGDOM — Dawah Through Character
**Time Period:** China, 7th–14th Century
**Color Palette:** Jade green, imperial red, gold, ink black, silk white, ocean blue
**Frames:** 0721–1080
**Dawah Thread:** Islam reached China through Muslim traders who were known for their honesty and character. They didn't conquer — they demonstrated. The Huaisheng Mosque was built during the Prophet's lifetime (peace be upon him). Zheng He, a devout Muslim, carried the Qur'an across seven seas.

### Scene 3A — The Muslim Traders of the Silk Road
**Frames:** 0721–0840

A caravan rests at a Silk Road oasis. Muslim traders perform wudu (ablution) at a fountain while Chinese merchants watch with interest. One trader unrolls a prayer mat and prays. His Chinese trade partner observes the discipline, the cleanliness, the devotion — and is moved. On the trader's pack, Arabic text is visible. The dawah is not a lecture — it's a life lived.

- **Camera:** Wide establishing shot of oasis, then intimate focus on the prayer and the Chinese merchant watching
- **Lighting:** Warm dappled light through oasis trees, dust particles in air
- **Key Elements:** Muslim trader performing wudu/prayer, Chinese merchant observing with respect, Silk Road caravan, prayer mat, Arabic text on goods, life-as-dawah moment

### Scene 3B — Zheng He and the Qur'an
**Frames:** 0841–0960

Admiral Zheng He on the deck of his massive flagship. He stands at the bow, looking out at the fleet of 300 ships. In his hands — a Qur'an. He reads from it as the wind catches the pages. He is a Muslim commanding the greatest fleet in history, and his faith guides his journey. Behind him, sailors perform group prayer on the deck.

- **Camera:** Over-shoulder shot of Zheng He reading Qur'an, revealing the fleet beyond, then wide to show deck prayer
- **Lighting:** Dawn light on ocean, golden reflections, wind in sails and Qur'an pages
- **Key Elements:** Zheng He with Qur'an, massive fleet in background, sailors praying on deck, Qur'an pages catching wind, faith guiding the journey

### Scene 3C — Sails to Arches Transition
**Frames:** 0961–1080

The billowing sails of Zheng He's fleet fill the frame — white and red silk catching wind. As the fabric ripples, Arabic calligraphy appears woven into the silk (the shahada or Qur'anic verse). The fabric folds harden into stone — the curves of the sails becoming the horseshoe arches of Moorish architecture. The message traveled from sea to stone.

- **Camera:** Macro on silk, pulling back as it transforms to architecture
- **Lighting:** Oceanic blue shifting to warm Mediterranean light
- **Key Elements:** Arabic text woven in silk, sails morphing to stone arches, calligraphy as connecting thread

## ERA 4: THE MOORS — Dawah Through Knowledge
**Time Period:** Al-Andalus & Europe, 8th–15th Century
**Color Palette:** Terracotta red, deep blue zellige tile, white marble, olive green, gold filigree
**Frames:** 1081–1440
**Dawah Thread:** The Moors didn't just conquer territory — they illuminated it. They translated the Qur'an's emphasis on knowledge ("Read!" — the first word revealed) into action. They built libraries, universities, and hospitals. Europeans came to learn, and many embraced Islam through exposure to its intellectual tradition.

### Scene 4A — The Qur'an in the Mosque of Cordoba
**Frames:** 1081–1200

The interior of the Great Mosque of Cordoba — the iconic red and white arches. But the focus is a Qur'an recitation in progress. A hafiz (one who has memorized the Qur'an) sits at the front, reciting from memory. Hundreds listen in the vast space, the arches carrying the sound. The architecture was built to amplify the Qur'an's recitation — form serving faith.

- **Camera:** Slow dolly through the arches toward the reciter, the columns framing the scene
- **Lighting:** Warm light through arches, the reciter in a pool of golden light
- **Key Elements:** Hafiz reciting Qur'an, listeners in the mosque, red/white arches amplifying the scene, Qur'anic connection to the architecture itself

### Scene 4B — Teaching the Sciences of the Qur'an
**Frames:** 1201–1320

A Moorish scholar teaches a mixed audience — some in Muslim dress, some European monks and scholars. On the table: Arabic translations of Greek texts alongside Qur'anic manuscripts. The scholar points to a mathematical formula, then to a Qur'anic verse about the heavens. The connection between scientific inquiry and faith is seamless. This is how dawah worked — through the brilliance of the Qur'an's worldview.

- **Camera:** Slow pan across the teaching scene, settling on the Qur'an and scientific text side by side
- **Lighting:** Warm candlelight, intimate scholarly atmosphere
- **Key Elements:** Qur'an alongside scientific texts, Moorish scholar teaching Europeans, mathematical instruments, faith and knowledge inseparable

### Scene 4C — Geometry to Tropics Transition
**Frames:** 1321–1440

Close on Qur'anic geometric patterns in zellige tilework — the mathematical perfection reflects the Qur'an's description of divine order. The camera pulls back. The geometric lines begin to loosen, becoming organic — straight edges curl into vines and leaves. The Qur'an's message of divine creation connects the mathematical Moors to the natural world of Southeast Asia. Tile becomes leaf, order becomes life.

- **Camera:** Macro pull-back, geometric to organic morph
- **Lighting:** Cool blue-white shifting to warm tropical green
- **Key Elements:** Qur'anic geometry in tiles, mathematical patterns becoming tropical foliage, divine order expressed in nature

## ERA 5: THE ISLAND ARCHIPELAGO — Dawah Through Living Example
**Time Period:** Southeast Asia, 13th–16th Century
**Color Palette:** Lush green, ocean turquoise, warm teak wood, sunset gold/orange
**Frames:** 1441–1800
**Dawah Thread:** This is the purest dawah story in history. No army. No conquest. Muslim traders simply lived the Qur'an — honest dealing, kindness, good character — and 230 million people in Indonesia alone embraced Islam. The Qur'an was taught in homes, shared in markets, lived in communities.

### Scene 5A — The Trader Shares the Qur'an
**Frames:** 1441–1560

A tropical harbor. A Muslim trader sits with a local Javanese family under a tree. He has a Qur'an open, teaching them to read Arabic. The children lean in, pointing at the letters. The trader's wife teaches the women nearby. It's intimate, organic — no formality, just neighbors sharing what is beautiful. Trade goods sit forgotten nearby. This is what changed a continent.

- **Camera:** Intimate medium shot of the teaching moment, warm and human
- **Lighting:** Dappled tropical shade, golden light filtering through leaves
- **Key Elements:** Qur'an open between trader and local family, children learning Arabic letters, women teaching women, trade goods in background, organic dawah moment

### Scene 5B — Community Prayer at the First Mosque
**Frames:** 1561–1680

Sunset over the ocean. A simple wooden mosque — the first in the village — stands among palm trees. The entire community gathers for prayer. The diverse congregation includes former Hindu and Buddhist locals alongside the Muslim traders who taught them. They stand shoulder to shoulder. The adhan (call to prayer) seems to echo across the water. 230 million Muslims trace back to moments like this.

- **Camera:** Wide establishing shot pulling toward the mosque, then the congregation in prayer
- **Lighting:** Full sunset, backlit silhouettes, the mosque framed against golden-orange sky
- **Key Elements:** Wooden mosque among palms, diverse congregation in unified prayer, sunset, adhan implied, shoulder-to-shoulder unity

### Scene 5C — Sunset to Sepia Transition
**Frames:** 1681–1800

The sunset scene holds — the congregation finishes prayer and rises. Slowly, the vibrant colors begin to drain. The image ages before your eyes — tropical color becoming sepia, the scene looking like a preserved memory. But the mosque remains visible even in sepia. The Qur'an's presence endures even as time moves forward. The final frames show a sepia ocean horizon.

- **Camera:** Static, the visual effect is the color transformation
- **Lighting:** Warm sunset desaturating to sepia/monochrome
- **Key Elements:** Prayer scene aging to sepia, mosque persisting through the color change, memory preserved

## ERA 6: THE NEW WORLD — Dawah Through Resilience
**Time Period:** Americas & the Modern Ummah, 15th Century–Today
**Color Palette:** Sepia dissolving into vibrant modern color
**Frames:** 1801–2160
**Dawah Thread:** Enslaved African Muslims brought the Qur'an with them in the only way they could — in their hearts. They wrote Qur'anic verses from memory, maintained their prayers, and passed the faith to their children. The Qur'an survived the Middle Passage. Today, Islam grows in the Americas because the message endures through every trial.

### Scene 6A — The Qur'an from Memory
**Frames:** 1801–1920

An enslaved African Muslim — Omar ibn Said or Bilali Muhammad — sits alone in dim light. With a stick or crude pen, he writes Arabic script on a wooden board or scrap of paper. It's a Qur'anic verse, written entirely from memory. He has nothing — no book, no mosque, no freedom — but the Qur'an lives in his heart and flows from his hand. The most powerful dawah: the Qur'an that cannot be taken away because it was memorized.

- **Camera:** Close on the hands writing Arabic, slow pull-back to reveal the man and his setting
- **Lighting:** Single light source (window/candle), dramatic chiaroscuro, warm on the writing hand
- **Key Elements:** Arabic Qur'anic text being written from memory, enslaved Muslim's hands, wooden board/paper, the Qur'an surviving captivity, dignity in the act of writing

### Scene 6B — Prayer Unbroken
**Frames:** 1921–2040

Silhouetted figures in sujood (prostration). Multiple eras layered — the enslaved Muslim from the previous scene dissolves into a freed man praying, then into a family praying in an early American mosque, then into a large diverse congregation today. The prayer position never changes. Through slavery, through freedom, through generations — the same sujood, the same Qur'an, the same connection to Allah. Unbroken.

- **Camera:** Slow dissolve between eras, the prayer posture constant as everything else changes
- **Lighting:** Darkness to light — each era brighter than the last, culminating in full modern light
- **Key Elements:** Sujood as visual constant, eras morphing around the unchanging prayer, darkness to light progression, the Qur'an's continuity across generations

### Scene 6C — The Global Ummah Today
**Frames:** 2041–2160

Rapid montage — a convert taking shahada with tears of joy, children in a weekend Islamic school reciting Qur'an, a Muslim doctor in hijab saving a life, a family breaking fast together, a new mosque being built in a Western city. Each shot is vibrant, full color — the Qur'an is alive and thriving today in every nation on earth. The montage accelerates, then the final frames wash to bright white.

- **Camera:** Cross-dissolve montage, each scene brighter and more vibrant
- **Lighting:** Each scene fully lit, modern, alive — the opposite of the dim historical scenes
- **Key Elements:** Shahada moment, Qur'an recitation, Muslim professionals, family ibadah, new mosques, the Qur'an alive in the modern world, color flooding the frame

## ERA 7: YOUR CHAPTER — The Dawah Continues
**Time Period:** Salik Foundation, Today
**Color Palette:** Salik blue (#4AB3E2), warm natural tones, white, soft earth tones
**Frames:** 2161–2520
**Dawah Thread:** Salik Foundation is the continuation of this 1000-year chain. Spreading the Qur'an. Caring for orphans (a Qur'anic command). Every viewer is being invited to take their place in this chain of dawah.

### Scene 7A — Qur'an Distribution
**Frames:** 2161–2280

Hands passing Qur'ans — a Salik Foundation volunteer placing a Qur'an into someone's hands for the first time. The recipient opens it with wonder. Cut to: stacks of Qur'ans being prepared for shipment, labeled for different countries. Cut to: a new Muslim reading their first Qur'an at home, sunlight on the page. The Qur'an Project in action — the same work that Timbuktu scribes did, that Silk Road traders did, that enslaved Muslims did from memory. The chain continues.

- **Camera:** Close on hands exchanging Qur'an, then wider scenes of distribution, then intimate first reading
- **Lighting:** Warm natural light, hopeful, present-day
- **Key Elements:** Qur'an being handed over, wonder on recipient's face, distribution operation, new Muslim reading, the chain of Qur'an transmission continuing today

### Scene 7B — Caring for the Orphan
**Frames:** 2281–2400

A Salik Foundation worker kneels to meet an orphan at eye level. The child smiles. The worker opens a school bag — inside is a Qur'an, school supplies, and food. This is the Qur'an's command made real: care for the orphan. Cut to: a classroom where sponsored children learn. Cut to: a child's hand on an open Qur'an. The two missions of Salik Foundation united — the Qur'an's message and the Qur'an's commands, both alive.

- **Camera:** Intimate, eye-level, warm human connection, close-ups of faces and hands
- **Lighting:** Golden hour outdoor, warm indoor classroom light
- **Key Elements:** Eye-level connection with orphan, Qur'an in school bag, sponsored children learning, child's hand on Qur'an, Salik blue accents, both missions united

### Scene 7C — You Are the Next Link
**Frames:** 2401–2520

Pull back to a wide shot — all the people Salik Foundation has served, a diverse community. Among them, the viewer can almost see echoes of the past: a Norseman's descendant, a Malian's descendant, a Chinese Muslim's descendant — all connected by the same Qur'an. The vignette slowly closes. The community fades into respectful darkness. The screen goes black. Silence. Then the Cinematic Closer begins (handled by code): "Vikings answered the call..."

- **Camera:** Wide establishing shot, slow vignette, fade to black
- **Lighting:** Warm golden hour dimming to black
- **Key Elements:** Diverse community united by the Qur'an, visual echoes of past eras, vignette to black, setting up the closer

The Qur'an is the thread that connects all 7 eras.
