# Salik Foundation — Cinematic Website Spec

> "You are not alone. You never were."

## Concept

A scroll-driven cinematic journey through 1000+ years showing how Islam reached every corner of the earth — not through a single people, but through Vikings, Norsemen, West Africans, Chinese, Southeast Asians, Europeans, and countless others who embraced the message. Each full-screen scene immerses the user in a different chapter, building the emotional truth: **Islam was never confined to one land or one people. Every nation, every culture, every continent has a chapter in this story — and so do you.**

The narrative starts ~900-1000 CE and moves forward, showcasing the incredible diversity of who became Muslim and how the message traveled through trade, exploration, scholarship, and human connection.

The site ends with a powerful closer that transforms the viewer from observer to participant.

## The 7 Eras

### Era 1: The Norsemen — Scandinavia, 10th Century
**Visual:** A Viking longship cutting through icy fjord waters at dawn. Norse warriors standing on a rocky shore. Among them — a man holding an object inscribed with Arabic script. Cold northern light, breath visible in the air.
**Visual Style:** AI-generated cinematic still
**Color Palette:** Steel grey, ice blue, dark wood, cold dawn light
**Historical Context:** Viking silver coins and burial artifacts inscribed with "Allah" and "Ali" found in Sweden. Norse traders encountered Islam through the Volga trade route with the Abbasid Caliphate. Ahmad ibn Fadlan documented his encounter with the Rus Vikings in 921 CE. A Viking ring found in a Birka grave was inscribed with Arabic Kufic script.
**Text Overlay:**
> "In the frozen north, where longships carved through ice..."
> "Vikings traded silver — and found something worth more than gold."

**Mood:** Unexpected, striking — Islam in the last place you'd expect
**Transition:** Ice and water dissolve into golden desert sand

---

### Era 2: The Empire of Gold — West Africa, 13th-14th Century
**Visual:** The grandeur of Timbuktu. Mansa Musa's caravan crossing the Sahara — so much gold it crashed the economies of every city he passed through. The great Djinguereber Mosque. Scholars in the Sankore University.
**Visual Style:** AI-generated cinematic still
**Color Palette:** Rich gold, deep earth brown, indigo, sunset amber
**Historical Context:** Mansa Musa of the Mali Empire was the wealthiest man in history. His 1324 hajj to Mecca with 60,000 men and 12,000 slaves each carrying 4 pounds of gold bars crashed gold prices across the Mediterranean. Timbuktu became a center of Islamic scholarship with 25,000 students. Columbus's son Ferdinand documented finding West African Mandinka spear tips and scarves on the shores of the Americas — evidence that Malian sailors crossed the Atlantic before European contact.
**Text Overlay:**
> "An empire so rich, one man's journey crashed the gold markets of three continents..."
> "And his sailors may have reached the Americas — centuries before Columbus."

**Mood:** Awe, power, the vastness of Muslim civilization most have never heard of
**Micro-CTA:** "They built empires of knowledge. Help build the next one." → Donate
**Transition:** Gold dust and sand swirl into ocean waves crossing the Atlantic

---

### Era 3: The Silk Road & the Middle Kingdom — China, 7th-14th Century
**Visual:** The ancient mosque of Xi'an (one of the oldest in the world). Chinese Muslim scholars in traditional dress. Zheng He's massive treasure fleet — 300 ships — dwarfing anything Europe had.
**Visual Style:** AI-generated cinematic still
**Color Palette:** Jade green, imperial red, gold, ink black, silk white
**Historical Context:** Islam reached China during the Tang Dynasty through trade routes. The Huaisheng Mosque in Guangzhou was built in 627 CE — during the Prophet's ﷺ lifetime. Admiral Zheng He, a Chinese Muslim, commanded the largest fleet the world had ever seen — 300 ships and 28,000 men — exploring Southeast Asia, India, Arabia, and East Africa decades before European "exploration." The Hui people became one of China's largest Muslim communities.
**Text Overlay:**
> "While Europe sailed in wooden boats, a Chinese Muslim admiral commanded 300 ships..."
> "The message traveled the Silk Road — and sailed the seven seas."

**Mood:** Scale, sophistication — shattering the myth that Islam was confined to the Middle East
**Micro-CTA:** "They carried the message across oceans. Carry it forward today." → Sponsor an Orphan
**Transition:** Ship sails billow and transform into the arches of Moorish architecture

---

### Era 4: The Moors — Al-Andalus & Europe, 8th-15th Century
**Visual:** The breathtaking interior of the Great Mosque of Cordoba. Moorish scholars in the libraries of Toledo. The Alhambra palace at sunset. Europeans studying under Muslim teachers.
**Visual Style:** AI-generated cinematic still
**Color Palette:** Terracotta, deep blue tile, white marble, olive green, gold filigree
**Historical Context:** Muslim Moors ruled much of the Iberian Peninsula for nearly 800 years, creating the most advanced civilization in medieval Europe. Cordoba had street lighting, running water, and 70 libraries when London was still a muddy village. European scholars traveled to Muslim Spain to study mathematics, astronomy, medicine, and philosophy. The words "algebra," "algorithm," "chemistry," and "zenith" all come from Arabic.
**Text Overlay:**
> "When Europe was in darkness, the Moors lit it with knowledge..."
> "The words you speak today — algebra, chemistry, zenith — are their legacy."

**Mood:** Intellectual dominance, beauty — the foundation of the European Renaissance came from Muslims
**Transition:** Geometric patterns fracture and reform into Southeast Asian wooden carvings

### Era 5: The Island Archipelago — Southeast Asia, 13th-16th Century
**Visual:** Muslim traders arriving at bustling port cities in Java, Sumatra, and Malacca. Wooden mosques rising among rice paddies and tropical forests. Communities gathering for prayer at sunset over the ocean.
**Visual Style:** AI-generated cinematic still
**Color Palette:** Lush green, ocean turquoise, warm teak wood, sunset gold
**Historical Context:** Islam spread across Southeast Asia almost entirely through trade, not conquest. Muslim merchants from Gujarat, Yemen, and China married into local communities and demonstrated Islam through their character and honest dealings. Today, Indonesia is the world's largest Muslim-majority nation — over 230 million Muslims — and it was never conquered by a Muslim army. Malaysia, Brunei, and the southern Philippines all embraced Islam through this peaceful, organic spread.
**Text Overlay:**
> "Not by the sword. Not by force. By character alone..."
> "Today, 230 million Muslims in one nation — because traders lived the message."

**Mood:** Peace, organic growth — the most powerful dawah is how you live
**Micro-CTA:** "They changed the world by example. Set yours today." → Volunteer
**Transition:** Ocean horizon blends into a modern cityscape emerging through morning mist

### Era 6: The New World — Americas & the Modern Ummah, 15th Century-Today
**Visual:** Transition montage — Malian sailors on Atlantic waters, enslaved African Muslims preserving their faith in the Americas, early mosques in North America, modern diverse Muslim communities in cities worldwide. Real photography blending in.
**Visual Style:** Mix — AI art for historical moments, stock footage for modern scenes
**Color Palette:** Sepia tones dissolving into vibrant modern color
**Historical Context:** Evidence suggests West African Muslims reached the Americas before Columbus — Ferdinand Columbus documented finding Mandinka trade goods. Enslaved African Muslims like Bilali Muhammad and Omar ibn Said preserved their faith and Arabic literacy in the Americas. Today, Islam is the fastest-growing religion in the world, with Muslims in every country on every continent — from Reykjavik to Rio, from Toronto to Tokyo.
**Text Overlay:**
> "They crossed oceans in chains — and still they prayed..."
> "From every nation. Every tongue. Every corner of the earth."

**Mood:** Resilience, arrival — the message cannot be stopped, it finds every people
**Micro-CTA:** "Across every border. Through every trial. The message endures." → Get Involved
**Transition:** Montage accelerates through modern scenes, then sudden stillness — screen brightens to white

## The Cinematic Closer

Full-screen takeover after Era 7. Dark background. Dramatic pause.

**Sequence:**
1. Screen goes dark — a beat of silence
2. Text fades in, large and centered:
   > "Vikings answered the call."
3. Pause. Then each line fades in, building:
   > "Emperors answered the call."
   > "Admirals answered the call."
   > "Scholars answered the call."
   > "Traders answered the call."
4. All text fades. A long pause. Then:
   > "Will you?"
5. The Salik Foundation logo materializes
6. Three action buttons emerge with subtle glow:
   - **Answer the Call** (primary, glowing) → Donate
   - **Sponsor an Orphan**
   - **Join the Chain**
7. Below: the tagline — "Guided by the Qur'an. Serving Humanity."

## Technical Approach

### Stack
- **Next.js 15** (App Router)
- **Tailwind CSS** for styling
- **Framer Motion** for scroll-triggered animations
- **GSAP ScrollTrigger** for parallax and pinned scenes
- **Intersection Observer** for era transitions and micro-CTA triggers

### Scroll Behavior
- Each era is a pinned full-viewport section
- Scroll progress within each era controls:
  - Parallax depth of background image
  - Text fade-in/out timing
  - Transition effects between eras
- Smooth, performant — no jank

### Performance
- Lazy-load images per era (only load when approaching)
- Compressed WebP/AVIF for AI-generated stills
- Optimized video with poster frames
- Preload next era as current one plays

### Responsive
- Full cinematic experience on desktop
- Simplified but still immersive on mobile (stacked scenes, swipe-friendly)
- Touch-friendly micro-CTAs
